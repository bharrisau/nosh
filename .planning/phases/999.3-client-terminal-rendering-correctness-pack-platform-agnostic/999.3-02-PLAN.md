<objective>
Fix the typematic / fast-typing glitch (D-02). Today, `classify_input` suppresses any stdin batch longer than `BULK_SUPPRESS_THRESHOLD` (4 bytes) as `BulkSuppressed`, so key-repeat or fast typing that coalesces into one read produces no prediction followed by a full repaint glitch in vim. Replace the flat length rule with **content inspection**: if the whole batch is printable single-width chars, predict each char in sequence; suppress only on escape/control/mixed/ambiguous content. Bracketed-paste and CJK behavior must be preserved.

Purpose: restore predictive echo for fast typists without re-introducing the paste/escape corruption the bulk rule was guarding against.
Output: modified `predictor.rs` with content-inspection classification and adversarial tests (D-06).

Note: this plan touches `predictor.rs` AFTER plan 999.3-01 (same file) — it depends on 01 to avoid merge conflict, not for logic. Rebase onto 01's `epoch_start_col` and `reset_with_cursor` changes.
</objective>
